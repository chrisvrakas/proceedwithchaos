"Like all the great profound truths in life are paradoxes. Any 2 points are infinitely different, any moment is perfectly unique" —Naval Ravikant

There is infinite wisdom inside of paradox. Logic is key to scientific truths, but reality is not bound to these confines. Many of life's most profound truths are hidden in-between the lines, obfuscated through paradox, and concealed from the plaintext realities of everyday life.

Everything in life is a contrast of complementary opposites - every negative holds within it a necessary positive, & vice versa — yin & yang, sun & rain, pleasure & pain, losses & gains.

"darkness and light are only meaningful in relationship with each other. without one, the other would not exist. they're a matched dynamic system like yin and yang" —Rick Rubin

You can't have one without the other, which is why much greatness in life comes from suffering.

If you're happy, that means you were sad at some point.
If she's attractive, that means someone else is unattractive.

These are not opposing conflicts to be resolved, but interdependent balances to be embraced.

Take for instance, any of the following —

  • less is more
  • change is the only constant
  • the more you fail, the more likely you are to succeed
  • the more you learn, the more you realize how little you know

At face value, each statement is counter-intuitive, and defies the standards of scientific logic and reason, yet real-life experience always resolves each of these contradictions into truth every time.

When you find two opposites that are both true, start exploring, and you'll discover how two seemingly contradictory statements align—equally correct—with a combined value that outweighs the sum of its parts.
